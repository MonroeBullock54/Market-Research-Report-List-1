<p><p>Cross Laminated Timber (CLT) is an engineered wood product made from layers of timber boards stacked crosswise and bonded together, enhancing strength and stability. The market for CLT is divided into two primary types: adhesive-bonded CLT, where layers are glued for durability and weather resistance, and mechanically fastened CLT, which uses screws or nails for assembly, allowing for easier disassembly and reconfiguration. Both types cater to diverse construction needs and preferences, offering sustainable building solutions.</p></p>
