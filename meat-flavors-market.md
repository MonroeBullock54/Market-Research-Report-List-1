<p><p>The meat flavors market is divided into two main types: natural and artificial meat flavoring. Natural meat flavoring is derived from real meat sources, including extracts and broths, providing authentic taste and aroma. It appeals to consumers seeking cleaner labels and healthier options. In contrast, artificial meat flavoring uses synthetic compounds to replicate meat flavors, often at a lower cost and with extended shelf life, making it popular in processed foods. Both types play significant roles in enhancing food products.</p></p>
