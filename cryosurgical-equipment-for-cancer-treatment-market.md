<p><p>Cryosurgical equipment for cancer treatment involves advanced cooling technologies to destroy cancer cells via extreme cold. The Phase Change Refrigeration method relies on substances that change phase from liquid to gas, absorbing heat and achieving low temperatures effectively for tissue ablation. In contrast, Joule-Thomson Throttling Refrigeration operates by expanding gas through a throttle, resulting in temperature drop, suitable for localized cryotherapy. Both methods are crucial for minimizing damage to surrounding tissues while targeting tumors in various cancers.</p></p>
