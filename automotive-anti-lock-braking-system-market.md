<p><p>The Automotive Anti-Lock Braking System (ABS) market is primarily categorized into two types: Pneumatic ABS and Hydraulic ABS. Pneumatic ABS uses air pressure to control the braking force, making it commonly suitable for heavy vehicles like trucks and buses. In contrast, Hydraulic ABS employs hydraulic fluid to modulate the braking pressure, providing more precise control and quicker response times, typically found in passenger cars. Both systems enhance vehicle safety by preventing wheel lock-up during braking, improving traction and control.</p></p>
