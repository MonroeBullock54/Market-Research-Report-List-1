<p><ul><li>Software Based Array</li><li>Hardware-based RAID</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Redundant Arrays of Independent Disks (RAID) can be categorized into software-based and hardware-based systems. Software-based RAID utilizes the operating system to manage disk arrays, offering flexibility and cost-effectiveness, but may consume system resources. In contrast, hardware-based RAID employs dedicated controllers to manage drives independently, enhancing performance and reliability, often with additional features like battery backup. Each type serves different needs, with software RAID being ideal for budget-conscious users and hardware RAID suited for performance-intensive applications.</p></p>
