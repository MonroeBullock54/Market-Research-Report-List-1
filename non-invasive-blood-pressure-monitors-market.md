<p><p>The Non-Invasive Blood Pressure Monitors market is primarily divided into two types: digital and mechanical. Digital monitors utilize electronic sensors and display readings on a screen, offering ease of use and rapid results, often with features like memory storage and Bluetooth connectivity. Mechanical monitors, also known as aneroid monitors, rely on traditional sphygmomanometer technology, requiring manual inflation and reading. While digital monitors are preferred for home use due to their convenience, mechanical monitors are often favored in clinical settings for their durability and reliability.</p></p>
