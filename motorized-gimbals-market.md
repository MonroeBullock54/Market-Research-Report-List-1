<p><p>Motorized gimbals are stabilization devices used to support cameras and other equipment, ensuring smooth footage during movement. The market is primarily divided into two types: 2-axis and 3-axis gimbals. </p><p>2-axis gimbals stabilize rotation along two axes, typically pitch and roll, making them suitable for basic stabilization needs. In contrast, 3-axis gimbals stabilize pitch, roll, and yaw, providing comprehensive stability and smoother results, which is essential for professional videography and filmmaking. This differentiation caters to various consumer and commercial needs.</p></p>
