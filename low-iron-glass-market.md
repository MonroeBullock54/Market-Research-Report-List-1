<p><ul><li>Rolled Glass</li><li>Float Glass</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The low-iron glass market comprises primarily two types: rolled glass and float glass. Rolled glass is produced by passing molten glass through rollers, resulting in a uniform thickness suitable for applications where clarity is essential. In contrast, float glass is made by floating molten glass on molten tin, creating a smooth, flawless surface that enhances transparency and optical quality. Both types are favored in architectural and automotive applications due to their superior light transmission properties and aesthetic appeal.</p></p>
