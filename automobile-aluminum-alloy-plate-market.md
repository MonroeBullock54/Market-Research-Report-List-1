<p><p>The automobile aluminum alloy plate market is primarily segmented into cold rolling and hot rolling types. Cold rolling involves the processing of aluminum at room temperature, resulting in improved surface finish and dimensional accuracy, making it ideal for applications requiring precise specifications. In contrast, hot rolling involves heating the aluminum above its recrystallization temperature, allowing for greater deformation and reduced thickness while enhancing ductility. Both methods cater to diverse automotive needs, balancing strength, weight, and manufacturing efficiency.</p></p>
