<p><p>The low frequency speakers market encompasses three main types: woofers, midwoofers, and subwoofers. Woofers are designed to reproduce low frequencies, typically from around 40 Hz to 1 kHz, providing depth and richness to sound. Midwoofers cover a broader range, usually from 50 Hz to 2 kHz, bridging the gap between bass and midrange frequencies. Subwoofers specialize in delivering ultra-low frequencies below 40 Hz, crucial for impactful bass in music and home theater applications, enhancing overall audio experience.</p></p>
