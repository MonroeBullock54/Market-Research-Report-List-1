<p><p>The automotive stamping market consists primarily of hot stamping and cold stamping processes. Hot stamping involves heating metal sheets before shaping, enhancing their strength and reducing weight for better fuel efficiency. This technique is ideal for high-strength components. In contrast, cold stamping shapes metal at room temperature, offering precision and faster production rates. It is commonly used for producing body panels and structural parts, focusing on cost-effectiveness and versatility in design. Both methods play crucial roles in automotive manufacturing.</p></p>
