<p><p>The lithium-ion power battery material market encompasses several key components. Cathode materials, often composed of lithium cobalt oxide or lithium iron phosphate, determine energy capacity and stability. Anode materials, primarily graphite or silicon, influence charge and discharge rates. Electrolytes, usually liquid or solid, facilitate ion movement between electrodes while ensuring safety and efficiency. Separators are critical for preventing short circuits, typically made from porous polymers. Each component's performance significantly affects the overall battery efficiency and longevity.</p></p>
