<p><ul><li>Polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE)</li><li>Polyvinylidene Fluoride (PVDF)</li><li>Fluorinated Ethylene Propylene (FEP)</li><li>Fluoroelastomers</li><li>Polyvinyl Fluoride (PVF)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The fluorine polymer market comprises several key types, each with unique properties and applications. Polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) is known for its exceptional chemical resistance and low friction, making it ideal for gaskets and seals. Polyvinylidene Fluoride (PVDF) offers high thermal stability and is often used in piping and coatings. Fluorinated Ethylene Propylene (FEP) is prized for its transparency and electrical insulation. Fluoroelastomers are flexible and withstand extreme temperatures, ideal for seals and O-rings. Polyvinyl Fluoride (PVF) is used in films and coatings for its durability. Other types include specialized variants that cater to niche applications.</p></p>
