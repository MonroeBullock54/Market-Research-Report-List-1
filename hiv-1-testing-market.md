<p><p>The HIV-1 testing market comprises three primary types: antibody testing, nucleic acid testing (NAT), and antigen testing. Antibody tests detect antibodies produced in response to HIV infection, offering a reliable method for diagnosis. Nucleic acid testing directly identifies the virus's genetic material, providing early detection and superior accuracy. Antigen testing detects specific proteins from the virus, helping to identify infections during the early acute phase. Each testing type serves distinct purposes in HIV-1 detection and monitoring, catering to various clinical needs.</p></p>
