<p><p>Bipolar Junction Transistors (BJTs) are classified into two main types: NPN and PNP. NPN transistors have a layer of p-type material sandwiched between two n-type layers, allowing current to flow from the collector to the emitter when a positive voltage is applied to the base. PNP transistors have the opposite configuration, with p-type material on the outer layers. Other BJT types include high-frequency and power BJTs, catering to specific applications in amplification and switching within electronic devices.</p></p>
