Near-infrared (NIR) and shortwave infrared (SWIR) detectors are primarily used in telecommunications and spectroscopy due to their sensitivity to wavelengths from 0.9 to 2.5 micrometers. Mid-wave infrared (MWIR) detectors operate between 3 and 5 micrometers, suitable for thermal imaging and environmental monitoring. Meanwhile, long-wave infrared (LWIR) detectors, functioning from 8 to 12 micrometers, excel in thermal imaging applications, crucial for surveillance and industrial inspections.</p></p>
