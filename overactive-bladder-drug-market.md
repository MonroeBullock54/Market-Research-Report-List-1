<p><p>The overactive bladder drug market is primarily categorized into anticholinergics and other specific medications. Solifenacin and fesoterodine are newer agents with improved tolerability. Trospium is another anticholinergic option, while other emerging treatments may include beta-3 adrenergic agonists and complementary therapies. This diverse range of medications offers various mechanisms to manage symptoms of overactive bladder, catering to different patient needs.</p></p>
