<p><p>The H. pylori noninvasive diagnostic tests market includes three primary types: Serology tests, Stool antigen tests, and Urea breath tests. Serology tests detect antibodies in the blood, indicating exposure to the bacteria, but may not distinguish between active and past infections. Stool antigen tests identify H. pylori antigens in stool samples, reflecting active infections. Urea breath tests measure metabolic byproducts of H. pylori after ingestion of a urea solution, providing rapid and accurate results. Each method offers distinct advantages for diagnosing infections.</p></p>
