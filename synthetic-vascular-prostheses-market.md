<p><p>The synthetic vascular prostheses market comprises various types of grafts used in vascular surgeries. ePTFE (expanded polytetrafluoroethylene) vascular grafts are known for their durability and biocompatibility, making them suitable for large-diameter applications. Polyester vascular grafts offer good strength and flexibility, often utilized in middle-sized vessel repairs. PTFE (polytetrafluoroethylene) vascular grafts are characterized by their low thrombogenicity and are used in a variety of vascular procedures. Other types may include materials like silicone and polyurethane, catering to specific clinical needs.</p></p>
