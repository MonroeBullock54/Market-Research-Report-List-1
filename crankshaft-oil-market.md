<p><p>The crankshaft oil market is categorized into liquid and semi-solid types based on viscosity and application. Liquid oils are low-viscosity, flowing easily to lubricate engine components, ideal for high-speed engines requiring efficient heat dissipation. Semi-solid oils, like greases, have higher viscosity, providing thicker lubrication and better adhesion, suitable for heavy-duty or low-speed engines where durability and protection against wear are critical. Both types ensure smooth crankshaft operation, reduce friction, and enhance engine performance, tailored to specific operational demands.</p></p>
